    Automatic summarization task has been a significant challenge in the Nature Process Language (NLP) filed. Since the deep neural networks were widely applied in various domains and achieved great success, the summarization has drawn much attention from recent research.
    The approach of dealing with automatic summarization can be roughly divided into two main paradigms: extractive and abstractive manner. The former allows capturing the most representative snippets in a document while the latter generates a summary by understanding the latent meaning in a material with a language generation model. However, in recent years, combining both paradigms to complement each other has been promoted.
    Accordingly, we adopt a reinforcement-based policy learning to bridge the nondifferentiable computation between these two neural networks in a hybrid way. We design two pseudo-agents to deal with the dilemma about saliency and redundancy. One agent receives snippets within a document and extracts the saliency; the other agent manages to judge whether snippets contain the redundancy. At first, we prepared two pre-trained models, including extractive and abstractive model. Our pseudo-agents conduct a specific policy over each model and attempt to maximize the total reward. Through cascading agents, not only is the model optimized globally by sharing parameters of observations; these agents are separately learning themselves.




  There is a variety of present ways with respect to the automatic summarization.
  Actually, the summarization also provides big gains in real world. For the journalist, it is vital to design a passage of the title on the news. Based on the title, we can obtain the most relevant information of content. In terms of the politician, to simplify and shorten the massive data where includes detailed questionnaires and online reviews supports to draw up the corresponding strategy. When it comes to the marketing, from the collections of reviews about the product, we can capture the customer psychology. Through summarizing reviews, an entrepreneur can develop a new market strategy. For the learner in cross-domain, it is difficult to entirely understand what jargon means, but through summarization system, the key digests will be extracted and help us to study efficiently. Therefore, automatic summarization can be applied in lots of aspects. With a general machine learning approach, we indeed alleviate much requirement of human-annotated data and human-engineering reading, so it results in more efficient and more flexible.

1. Human-Subjectivity
    Text summarization is to produce a summary of the main ideas of the text dependent on an individual. The fact is that opinions vary from person to person.




2. Local Relevance
    For the corpus that constitutes of article-summary pairs, such CNN/DM, BBC and NYT. Most of words in summary are mentioned on the corresponding article.




3. Global Independence
    If we want to collect side document to improve the quality of generated summary, these words occurring across the document provide a relevant signal for content understanding. These side document commonly having a similar topic extend relevant information that can assist in grasping the content meaning sooner and more comprehensive.

    We attempt to make extractor and abstractor complement each other. The former extractor allows capturing the most representative snippets in document and the latter abstractor generates summary by filtering redundancy inside snippets. However, most of traditional approaches that applied the hybrid structure were word-level sequential model which were attempt to find the similar words in gold summary from vocabulary distribution. We find that the sequential models are not the good solution in summarization task. Due to the nature of discrete in word, with the sequential model being difficult to fit a distinct sentence distribution, sentence composed of several words leads to cast to a diverse space.
